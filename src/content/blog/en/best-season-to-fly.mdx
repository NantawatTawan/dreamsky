Chiang Mai has three distinct seasons, and each gives you a completely different flying experience.

## Cool season (November – February)

The best window of the year. Crisp air, clear skies, and fantastic visibility. On some mornings you'll drift above a sea of mist pooling in the valleys — a rare sight.

**Best window:** Early morning, for golden light and morning mist.

## Hot season (March – May)

It's warm, but early morning and late afternoon flights are still great. Haze can appear in March and April — we reschedule if air quality is poor.

**Best window:** Late afternoon, for a gold-orange sunset.

## Rainy season (June – October)

Rice fields turn vivid green and the air feels fresh after rain, but the weather needs close watching. Some days flying isn't possible.

**Tip:** Book flexibly — we'll reschedule if it rains.

## The bottom line

For the most reliable window, aim for **November – February**. Every season has its own character, though — pick what works for your trip and come fly with us.
